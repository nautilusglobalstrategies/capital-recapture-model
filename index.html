Capital Recapture Model

A decision tool built around the Capital Recapture Engine™ to model how capital can be recaptured, redeployed, and cycled to accelerate debt elimination.

Purpose: compare a standard payoff path against a structured recapture cycle using Level 1 capital, deposit timing, and recurring obligations.

Inputs

Enter your deposits, monthly obligations, debt profile, and Level 1 assumptions.

Income Deposits

Deposits are modeled by frequency: weekly = every 7 days, bi-weekly = every 14 days, monthly = every 30 days.

Monthly Obligations

Monthly obligations are withdrawn once per month on day 30, after deposits that day. Do not include the primary debt payment here.

Primary Debt

Primary debt interest is modeled monthly using 30-day months. Standard payment is applied on the selected day.

Level 1 Capital Layer

Level 1 represents the first capital layer in the recapture cycle. A new deployment is only made after Level 1 is paid back down near zero.

How to use the model

This page is built to help serious users think more clearly about structure, flow, and recapture timing.

What this models

This model compares a standard payoff path against the Capital Recapture Engine™, where capital is repeatedly deployed and recaptured through a structured cycle.

Core drivers

Cash Flow Strength
The stronger the gap between deposits and monthly obligations, the faster Level 1 can be recaptured.
Deployment Size
Larger deployments may reduce principal faster, but they can also increase carry and slow the reset cycle.
Timing Matters
The model uses fixed timing assumptions. Real-world posting schedules and account behavior can affect outcomes.

Why “Level 1”

Level 1 is intentionally generic. In a broader strategy, multiple layers of capital may be involved, including liquidity reserves, cash-equivalent accounts, or other structured capital sources.

Best use

  • Compare strategic payoff paths before making a move
  • Test whether your cash flow can sustain a recapture cycle
  • Model how redeployment speed changes payoff outcomes

Important note

This is a planning model. It is useful for strategy comparison, but it does not replace lender-specific amortization, posting rules, or formal underwriting review.
